## 2. Two material findings from runtime verification

The shortlist hand-off prompted fresh verification that **changed two cost/feasibility lines** versus the framework:

### 2.1 ✅ SDA flags resolved
- **Birgu "Cottonera Development" IS a statutory SDA — but narrowly.** It designates the *specific named regeneration-project units*, **not** the ordinary houses of character in Birgu's historic core that Candidate 4 targets. So the realistic Birgu buy is **non-SDA (Route B/C)** — *unless the specific listing falls inside the Cottonera Development polygon*, in which case it flips to SDA (Route A, interim let available). **The notary must confirm the exact boundary against the address before any interim-let income is credited.**
- **Tas-Sellum Residence AND Southridge (Mellieħa) confirmed SDA.** This gives Candidate 3 a genuine **clean early-let route (3b)** — at the cost of switching from the detached villa to an apartment/maisonette.
- **Three Villages: confirmed no SDA** (Madliena Village SDA is not in the villages). **Gozo honourable mention: confirmed non-SDA** (Gozo SDAs are Fort Chambray, Kempinski San Lawrenz, Vista Point — none in Victoria/Gharb/Sannat).

### 2.2 🟢 UCA stamp-duty relief — a large cost saving the framework had not captured
Properties inside an **Urban Conservation Area** (almost all the houses of character — Rabat/Mdina, Three Villages, Birgu historic core) qualify for **0% stamp duty on the first €750,000 and 1.5% on the next €200,000** — roughly **halving the duty bill** versus the flat 5% the market memo budgeted, **plus an 18% VAT refund on the first €300k of restoration spend**. This cuts *for* the character homes and *against* the SDA apartments (Sliema/St Julian's, Tas-Sellum), which sit outside UCAs and pay the full **5%**. *Confirm per-address UCA status and obtain the PA UCA Declaration Letter pre-deed.* **The duty economics now favour exactly the homes the family wants** — a point for Investment Strategy ([MAL-5](/MAL/issues/MAL-5)) to fold into the like-for-like costing.

## 3. The interim-let universe, after verification

Exactly **three options** in the shortlist support a clean buy-early-and-let interim:

1. **(5) Sliema/St Julian's SDA penthouse** — the cleanest, and the only one that works **today even if Vicki's citizenship slips**. Long-let to the i-gaming/fintech pool is the lowest-void interim carry on the island.
2. **(3b) Tas-Sellum/Southridge SDA apartment in Mellieħa** — clean early-let, but an apartment-in-block product with condominium/common-parts and possible locality-cap risk on the short-let side.
3. **(4-contingent) a Birgu unit inside the Cottonera Development SDA polygon** — only if the boundary check confirms it; otherwise non-SDA.

All three are **apartment-type products carrying full 5% duty (no UCA relief)**. **Long-let is the recommended interim mode** for any of them — simpler compliance, single tenant for the window, clean vacant possession at occupation, and it avoids the LN 92/2026 short-let exposure (see §4). Short-let upside remains **unbanked until licensing is cleared per property**.

For the **character homes (Routes B/C)**, the interim question is moot — they cannot be let — so the decision is **defer the purchase (Route B: rent a home meanwhile, buy clean as a citizen at ~2029)** vs **buy early and hold unlet (Route C: home-plus-appreciation, but conflicts with the client's "never vacant" preference, Q13)**. Route B is the cleaner fit given the client is open to renting in the interim (Q12) and is conservative (won't move until school finishes).

**Getting the home back at the ~2029 move — Ch. 604 lease structuring (interim-let candidates 3b/5 only).** A Maltese long-let under the Private Residential Leases Act (Ch. 604) must be **≥1 year** and expires cleanly at the end of its fixed term **only if the landlord serves ≥3 months' notice before expiry** — absent that notice it **auto-renews for a further year**. So an interim let on 3b/5 frees the home on time **provided** the lease is (i) a fixed term aligned to the school-end/move date and (ii) diarised for the mandatory 3-month termination notice. This is a structuring instruction for the letting agent, not a blocker. *(Verified at runtime: [Cap. 604](https://rentregistration.mt/wp-content/uploads/2020/01/Cap-604-of-2019.pdf), [CSB Group](https://www.csbgroup.com/malta-news/real-estate/a-guide-to-the-2020-rent-regulations-the-private-residential-leases-act/), [Expatax notice periods](https://expatax.mt/ending-a-rental-contract-in-malta-notice-periods/).)*

Note the corollary the framing turns on: the **short-let "optionality" Market Research flagged on the non-SDA homes (Rabat/Mdina, Birgu) is *not* an interim-window play** — those homes cannot be let at all pre-occupation; free letting unlocks only at 5 years' citizen residence, by which point the family is living in the house. Treat any such short-let as a much-later, post-settlement option, never as interim income.

## 4. Short-let licensability (LN 92 of 2026) — per-property flags

Short-let is only on the table for an **SDA** unit (the only one that can be let interim). For those, the MTA Holiday Furnished Premises licence under **Legal Notice 92 of 2026** gates on:

- **Planning conformity / valid development permission** — the licence is refused without it.
- **No bedrooms in basements/underground**; **≤2 persons/bedroom, ≤10 total** unless independent street access; **no single booking >90 days**; **high standard of finish**; **€250k+ liability insurance incl. common parts**; a **designated 24/7 contact person**.
- **Unlicensed operation = 3-year disqualification attaching to BOTH operator AND the property address.**
- **Apartments-in-blocks are the highest-risk format** (condominium/common-parts consent and block-level practical risk) — this hits **3b and 5** directly.
- **MTA may set locality caps** in saturated zones — **Sliema/St Julian's (5) is the most exposed**; Mellieħa (3b) is a beach-tourism zone with some exposure.
- **The 24/7 contact person cannot be the Kents from Melbourne** — the letting/management agent must contractually hold that role (a hard requirement, per Settlement annex §5).

**Net:** short-let is licensable-but-conditional on the SDA units and operationally heavier; **long-let is the lower-risk interim default**. Investment Strategy should model long-let net for 3b/5 and treat any short-let premium as upside, not base case.

## 5. James's residence route & the family-home acquisition (confirmed)

- **Residence:** James (Australian + post-Brexit British = third-country national) qualifies for a **family-member eResidence card** as the spouse of an EU/Maltese citizen under **Directive 2004/38/EC**, via Identità's Expatriates Unit — residence + right to work without a separate permit + Schengen travel. ~**30 working days** once filed; needs an **apostilled marriage certificate** and **€100k+ Maltese health insurance**. **Vicki must register her own EU position first** (economic self-sufficiency is the obvious basis given the post-sale liquidity) — she gates James's card.
- **The family-home purchase:** James does **not** hold the citizen property exemption himself. On a joint purchase of the primary residence, the exemption relied on is **Vicki's**. **The notary must confirm joint-acquisition/spousal-acquisition treatment so James's TCN status does not re-trigger an AIP requirement on each non-SDA home.** *Inside any SDA (5, 3b, a Cottonera-polygon unit) this is moot — no permit for anyone* — a quiet point in favour of the SDA routes for a mixed-nationality couple.

## 6. The gating dependency — 1 August 2028 citizenship cliff (unchanged, escalated)

The entire base case ("Vicki as a registered Maltese citizen") depends on her **mother's citizenship registration** being completed before the **1 Aug 2028 descent cliff** (where the qualifying ancestor was alive on 1 Aug 2007, the descent link breaks if not registered before the ancestor dies after that date). This is **specialist citizenship counsel's work, outside our remit, but it gates the whole property regime** — without registered citizenship, the case collapses to the pre-citizenship (both-TCN) column, where the home itself needs AIP or SDA, and James's residence route also falls away.

**Open item O2 remains unconfirmed:** the client is "meeting Komunità Malta" (the government agency, not necessarily the counsel managing the *mother's* registration). **Recommendation, to be put to the client now: confirm a specialist citizenship lawyer is retained on Vicki's mother's registration.** This is the most time-critical dependency in the file.

**Settlement sequencing note:** the family does **not** need a Maltese bank account to complete a purchase — funds wire from Australia directly to the **notary's client account** (10% escrow at konvenju, balance at deed). So "waiting for citizenship for banking" (Q30) does **not** block an early purchase. Source-of-funds documentation on the ~€1M inflow from the A$5–7M sale **must be assembled contemporaneously with the sale** (the largest AML exposure in the file), and FX on AUD→EUR is a real ~€10k+ cost/hedging line for Investment Strategy.
