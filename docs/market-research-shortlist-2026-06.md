## 1. The one decision that frames the whole shortlist

At €1M the binding trade-off is **a real home with outdoor space vs. a premium harbour address**, and it collides with a legal fact:

- **The home the family actually wants** — a characterful house with a courtyard/yard/garden, guest space, walkable to a village square — is almost always **non-SDA** (ordinary stock). A €1M harbour budget, by contrast, buys only a 2–3-bed **penthouse with a terrace, no garden** (a 3-bed Sliema penthouse already starts ~€1.25M).
- **Non-SDA + the ~3-year interim let is the legally awkward part.** A non-EU buyer's AIP-permit property is for personal use and generally **cannot be let**. The clean route to *buy-early-and-let* a village house is **Vicki's EU citizenship landing before purchase** (a citizen's primary-residence purchase needs no permit; free letting follows after 5 years' residence). **SDA properties let freely in either regime, immediately** — but are the wrong *product* (apartments/penthouses, no garden, turnkey).

**So every candidate below is tagged SDA / non-SDA, and the interim-let availability is flagged for [@Director of Relocation & Legal](agent://9ecf0066-1b79-462d-b08c-988f329d0bf2) to classify per property.** This is the gate on whether "buy early" is even available — it is property-specific, and it is the legal team's call, not ours.

## 2. Interim-let strategy: long-let, not short-let

The Rental Yield Analyst's clear recommendation: **model every candidate on long-let for the full interim.** Two reasons:

1. **At a €1M price point, gross yield mechanically compresses to ~2.5–3.8%** regardless of strategy — rents don't scale linearly with price (a €1M home does not command 3× the rent of a €330k flat). Published 4–5% Malta yields are for small cheap units. Long-let still **broadly self-carries**, which is all the mandate asks (income = enabler of buying early, not a return play). Net ≈ gross − ~1.5 pts after agent + costs.
2. **Short-let is now actively risky as a primary plan.** **Legal Notice 92 of 2026** (Tourist Accommodation Regs; published 15 Apr 2026, in force ~mid-June 2026) is the largest STR overhaul in years: mandatory MTA Holiday Furnished Premises licence gated on **planning conformity**; EU Reg 2024/1028 (from 20 May 2026) forces Airbnb/Booking to verify registration numbers; **unlicensed operation = 3-year disqualification attaching to BOTH operator AND the property address**; MTA may now impose **locality-specific caps** (saturated harbour zones most at risk); apartments-in-blocks are the highest-risk format. **A yield that depends on an unlicensable short-let is not a yield** — any short-let upside is *unbanked* until Legal clears it (full flag list in §5).
[VB Advocates](https://www.vba.mt/2026/04/23/operating-short-lets-in-malta-heres-what-the-2026-regulations-change/) · [Casa Rooms — LN 92/2026](https://casarooms.com/malta-short-let-regulations-2026/) · [MTA HFP licence](https://mta.com.mt/application-for-a-holiday-furnished-premises-licence/)

**Acquisition-cost reality (keeps all-in ≤ €1M):** mainland stamp duty **5%** + notary/searches ~2% → work to an **asking ceiling ~€925k**. Gozo stamp duty is **2%**, so the budget stretches further there (asking ~€955–965k still lands under €1M all-in). Fit-out is outside the €1M per mandate. [Malta stamp duty 2026](https://maltacalculator.com/blog/malta-stamp-duty-complete-guide-2026)

**Macro tailwind for buying early:** NSO house-price index **+6.1% y/y (Q1 2026)**; eurozone-cheap mortgages (~2.0–2.1%); the village house / house-of-character segment is supply-constrained (new permits are ~86% apartments in the harbour). [NSO RPPI](https://nso.gov.mt/property/)

## 3. The shortlist — five candidates

Ranked by overall fit to the **home-first** mandate, with the legally-cleanest interim-let option included last as a deliberate fallback. Prices are June-2026 **asking**.

### Candidate 1 — Rabat / Mdina · *house of character* — **TOP PICK (home-fit)**
- **SDA status:** 🔵 **Non-SDA.** Interim let depends on the citizenship/permit route → **Legal to classify.**
- **Indicative price:** Converted 3-bed houses of character **€690k–€905k** — comfortably under the €925k ceiling with renovation headroom. [propertymarket.com.mt Rabat HoC](https://www.propertymarket.com.mt/for-sale/houses-of-character-in-rabat/) · [Frank Salt Rabat](https://franksalt.com.mt/rabat/houses-for-sale-in-rabat/)
- **Home case:** The best all-round fit. Atmospheric historic town wrapping the silent city of Mdina; HoC stock comes with **courtyards / internal yards / roof terraces** (real outdoor space), **3–4 beds** (strong guest capacity), and a **genuinely walkable core** — Saqqajja / Parish Square restaurants plus Mdina's dining a short walk. Outstanding renovation/value-add market (the classic "restore a shell" play — suits James the handyman).
- **Interim-let case:** **~3.0–3.8% gross** long-let to families/professionals; *plus* genuine Mdina-tourist **short-let** optionality (if and only if Legal clears licensing). Dual-channel flexibility is a real edge here.

### Candidate 2 — Three Villages: Attard / Balzan / Lija · *house of character* — **prestige home-fit**
- **SDA status:** 🔵 **Non-SDA** (the adjacent Madliena Village SDA is *not* in the villages). → **Legal to classify** the interim let.
- **Indicative price:** 3-bed HoC with courtyard **€830k–€850k**; restored 4-beds with mature garden + pool trade above €1M, so ~budget buys a characterful 3-bed with courtyard/garden, possibly light works. [propertymarket.com.mt 3-Villages HoC](https://www.propertymarket.com.mt/for-sale/?li=187%2C131%2C134%2C139%2C182&pc=3&pt=42) · [Christie's 3 Villages guide](https://christiesrealestatemalta.com/news/a-guide-to-the-3-villages-balzan-attard-lija)
- **Home case:** Malta's most prestigious inland address — leafy, quiet, period palazzos in Urban Conservation Areas, **gardens/courtyards realistic at budget**, 3–4 beds for guests. Walkable **Balzan and Lija village squares** with parish churches and a handful of restaurants (residential rather than restaurant-dense — meets the walkability criterion but is not a nightlife strip). Excellent renovation upside.
- **Interim-let case:** **~3.0–3.8% gross**, solid long-let to professional families / corporate tenants who want space and quiet. Long-let is the right mode (thin short-let market). Self-carries.

### Candidate 3 — Mellieħa · *detached villa with garden + pool* — **the garden/guests maximiser**
- **SDA status:** 🔵 **Non-SDA** core **+ an SDA escape hatch: Tas-Sellum Residence** (and Southridge) within Mellieħa let cleanly in either regime → a route to a **clean interim let without waiting on citizenship**, at the cost of the detached-villa product. → **Legal to classify both routes.**
- **Indicative price:** Villas **start ~€1M** — this is the **entry of the villa-with-garden market**, not the middle. A 4-bed villa with pool/garden/sea views sits around budget. SDA apartments/maisonettes at Tas-Sellum sit below. [Frank Salt Mellieħa villas](https://franksalt.com.mt/mellieha/villas-for-sale-in-mellieha/) · [Sotheby's Mellieħa](https://www.maltasothebysrealty.com/property-for-sale/in/mellieha)
- **Home case:** The only realistic route at ~€1M to a **true detached villa with garden and pool** — best outdoor space and guest capacity (4 beds) of the Malta options, hilltop sea views, family-friendly, beaches nearby. Trade-off: villas sit on the periphery, so it is **car-dependent to reach the walkable town square** (partial on walkability).
- **Interim-let case:** **~2.8–3.5% gross**; steady long-let plus strong seasonal short-let (beach tourism, subject to licensing). Thinner year-round long-let than central towns — model long-let conservatively.

### Candidate 4 — Birgu (Vittoriosa) / Three Cities · *house of character + waterfront* — **character + water, distinctive**
- **SDA status:** 🔵 **Non-SDA** (core stock ordinary; "Cottonera Development" appears on one SDA list — verify the specific property). → **Legal to classify.**
- **Indicative price:** Houses of character **€870k–€895k** with roof/front terraces and marina views (a restored 16th-c. palazzo runs to ~€1.95M, well above budget). [propertymarket.com.mt Three Cities HoC](https://www.propertymarket.com.mt/for-sale/?li=224%2C241%2C207%2C145&pc=3&pt=42)
- **Home case:** Grand Harbour history, palazzos, the buzzing **Vittoriosa Waterfront marina** (wine bars, restaurants) — increasingly fashionable, highly walkable, characterful, well below Sliema prices. 3–4 beds for guests; strong renovation/value-add. **Caveat:** dense historic fabric means **terraces/roof terraces and small courtyards rather than true gardens** — outdoor space yes, large garden unlikely.
- **Interim-let case:** **~3.0–3.8% gross**; growing long-let *and* short-let demand (waterfront/marina/tourism) — a real and rising market. Long-let recommended for the interim; short-let optional subject to licensing.

### Candidate 5 — Sliema / St Julian's **via SDA** (Tigné Point / Portomaso / Pendergardens) · *penthouse* — **the clean-let fallback**
- **SDA status:** 🟢 **SDA — lets freely in either regime, immediately, no 5-year wait, no AIP permit.** This is the **legally cleanest interim-let** of the five and the only one that works *today* even if Vicki's citizenship slips.
- **Indicative price:** ~€700k–€1M+ for a **2–3-bed penthouse with a 45+ sqm terrace**, sea/valley views, modern block, turnkey. [propertymarket.com.mt penthouses](https://www.propertymarket.com.mt/for-sale/?li=226%2C228&pc=101&pt=29)
- **Home case:** **Weakest on the home brief** — terrace only (no garden/yard), 2–3 beds (tight for "family & friends"), not a renovation play. But best-in-class walkability (Spinola/Balluta/seafront) and the most liquid market on the island.
- **Interim-let case:** **~2.6–3.5% gross** but the **deepest tenant pool and lowest void risk** in Malta (i-gaming/fintech; "demand exceeds supply"). Most reliable self-carry.
- **When to choose it:** Recommend **only if Legal confirms the interim let cannot be made clean on a non-SDA home and that constraint outweighs the garden** — i.e. if buying before citizenship is registered is the priority.

**Honourable mention — Gozo (Victoria / Gharb / Sannat) farmhouse with pool + garden (€675k–€785k):** unbeatable outdoor-space-and-guests value, a walkable square in Victoria (It-Tokk / St George's), 2% stamp duty stretches the budget. Ranked off the main list only because the client stated **Malta over Gozo** and the interim long-let market is thin (leans seasonal short-let, which the new licensing makes a weaker carry). Non-SDA; SDAs are Fort Chambray / Kempinski / Vista Point. Keep live if the family warms to Gozo.
