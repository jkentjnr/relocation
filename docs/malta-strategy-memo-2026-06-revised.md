## 3. The core decision: citizenship + the interim-let, restated for one property

Maltese law treats *living in* a property and *renting it out* very differently — and treats *you* differently by citizenship. For a single property that you might buy early, let, then occupy, the whole question turns on **one legally-classified point**, and our Legal team has now classified it per candidate.

### 3.1 The two regimes

- **Pre-citizenship (both third-country nationals).** James's British passport is **not** an EU status post-Brexit, so today you are *both* TCNs. Routes: an **AIP-permit** property (one property, must be your residence, **letting not permitted**), or an **SDA** property (no permit, **may let**, any purpose — but a premium apartment/penthouse product).
- **Post-citizenship (Vicki registered).** Vicki's **primary residence needs no AIP permit and is not confined to SDAs** — buy *anywhere*. James gains **family-member residence rights** as her spouse. **But** free *letting* of a non-SDA property unlocks only after **5 years' continuous residence** — which you cannot start early (Q11: "No").

### 3.2 The finding that decides the file

> **Citizenship changes *where* the home can be — not *whether* it can be let early.** The citizen's exemption attaches to a property you *actually occupy as your home*. A property bought early and let for ~3 years before you move in is, during that window, **not yet your primary residence** — so neither regime lets you let a character home in the interim. **Only an SDA property lets cleanly in the interim, in either regime, immediately.**

Legal's per-property verdict (full classification in `feasibility-livability-classification-2026-06.md`):

|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Rabat/Mdina** house of character | Non-SDA | ✅ | ❌ No (either regime) | B / C |
| 2 | **Three Villages** house of character | Non-SDA | ✅ | ❌ No | B / C |
| 3a | **Mellieħa** detached villa | Non-SDA | ✅ | ❌ No | B / C |

## 5. The numbers — acquisition, tax & the cost of waiting

### 5.1 Acquisition cost — and a relief that favours the home you want

All figures re-verified at runtime 2026-06-19 (sources §9). Baseline all-in ≈ **5% stamp + ~1.6% notary/searches (+ €233 AIP only on a non-SDA TCN purchase)**. Agency commission is **vendor-paid → ~€0 to you**. Malta levies **no annual property/council tax** (confirm ground rent / ċens — freehold vs leasehold — on title).

**The key cost finding cuts *for* the character home:**
- **Urban Conservation Area (UCA) relief** — almost all houses of character (Three Villages, Rabat/Mdina, Birgu core) qualify for **0% stamp duty on the first €750,000, 1.5% on the next €200k** (a saving of up to ~€37.5k), **plus an 18% VAT refund on the first €300k of restoration**. The owner-occupier **3.5% band on the first €200k** also applies. The SDA apartments (Sliema, Tas-Sellum) sit *outside* UCAs and pay the **full 5%, no residence relief** (an interim-let unit is bought as an investment, not a residence).
- **Net effect:** the "income" route starts ~**€35–40k behind on tax** before earning a euro, and **the duty economics favour exactly the homes you want.**
- ⚠️ **Timing trap:** the UCA first-€750k exemption **sunsets 31 Dec 2026** (subject to budget renewal). A 2029 purchase likely *won't* get it — capturing it would mean buying a character home in 2026 and eating ~3 years of empty-house carry, which the numbers say is **not worth it** (§5.3). The **3.5% owner-occupier band is permanent** and still applies in 2029.

### 5.3 The cost of waiting — home-plus-appreciation (the five non-lettable homes)

For the character homes there is no interim rent; the only lever is *when* to buy.

**Route B — buy at occupation (~2029), as Vicki's citizen primary residence (recommended).** No permit, buy anywhere, 3.5% residence band, cash (or optional 90% citizen-LTV). No carry, no vacant-home risk, respects "never vacant." **You forgo ~3 years of appreciation:**

**Route C — buy early (2026), hold empty 3 years.** Locks today's price and the specific property and can bag the UCA exemption (~€37.5k) — but standby costs (~€12k/yr) **plus** the opportunity cost of ~€1M deployed early (~€40k/yr) ≈ **€150–156k of carry over 3 years**, roughly cancelling the appreciation, *and* it violates "never vacant" (Q13) and drags in the AIP-secondary route. **Not recommended.**
