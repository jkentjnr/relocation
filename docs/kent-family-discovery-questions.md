## 1. Objectives, priorities & what "success" looks like

*Why this matters:* the brief tells us the **home is the end goal** and **rental income is important** — but "important" can mean "a strong preference we'd trade away for the right home" or "a hard requirement the plan must hit." The two lead to different shortlists.

3. **🔑 The home-vs-income trade-off.** If the single best *home* for your family absorbed most or all of the budget and left no room for a separate income property, would you (a) take the home anyway, (b) accept a more modest home to free up cash for an income unit, or (c) only proceed if both goals are met?
4. **What does a successful outcome look like in 5 years?** Primarily a place you love to live? A portfolio that is also building wealth? A base that simplifies the Australian business tax position? Rank these if you can.
5. **Income target.** Is there a number or yield you are aiming for from rental property (e.g. "cover the running costs," "€X net per year," "a target % return"), or is any reasonable income welcome?
6. **Capital growth vs cash yield.** Would you rather a property that earns steady rent now, or one more likely to appreciate (even if it earns little or nothing today)? This decides how we weight non-rentable "home-plus-appreciation" options.
7. **Exit horizon.** Is this a forever move, or do you foresee selling/returning within, say, 10 years? Maltese exit costs and CGT treatment differ by holding period and by whether it was your residence.

## 2. Citizenship & residency timing — the hinge of the whole engagement 🔑

*Why this matters:* a change from third-country national to EU/Maltese citizen **largely dissolves the property-permit (AIP) problem for a primary residence**. Timing is therefore a first-class variable, and there is a hard legal deadline in play. These questions sit with specialist citizenship counsel (outside our remit) but we need the facts to time everything else.

8. **🔑 Where is Vicki's citizenship-by-descent application right now?** Has her **mother** registered as a Maltese citizen yet (the living link), or is that still to be done? Has Vicki's own application been lodged? What have you been told about the timeline to completion?
9. **🔑 The 2028 cliff.** Are you aware of, and acting on, the risk that if the qualifying parent (Vicki's mother) was alive on 1 Aug 2007 and dies after **1 Aug 2028 without registering**, the descent link can break? Do you have a specialist citizenship lawyer engaged on the mother's registration *now*? This is the most time-critical item in the file.
10. **James's residence route.** Are you relying on family-member residence rights as the spouse of an EU citizen (post-registration), or pursuing a separate residence/visa route in the meantime (e.g. as a remote business owner)? Do you hold, or have you ever held, any other EU residence status?
11. **The 5-year clock.** A Maltese citizen may buy *freely for any purpose, including letting,* after **5 years' continuous residence**. Given the children finish school in ~3.5 years, when do you each expect to first establish continuous Maltese residence? Could either of you start the residence clock *earlier* than the family move?
12. **Willingness to wait.** Would you be comfortable **deferring the home purchase** until Vicki's citizenship is registered (to buy anywhere, with no SDA restriction and better mortgage terms), or do you want the option to transact sooner even if it constrains the choice?

## 3. Timing, the move sequence & the family

*Why this matters:* the brief says the children finish school in ~3.5 years and you "would start to relocate" then. The gap between *buying* and *moving* affects financing, letting, and whether a property sits empty.

13. **The phased timeline.** Is the plan to **buy now and move in ~3.5 years**, or to buy closer to the move? If you buy early, would the home be let, lent, or left vacant in the interim — and does that change which property works?
14. **Children.** How many, what ages, and what are their schooling needs in Malta (international vs state, location, start dates)? School catchment often drives the *home* location more than any investment metric.
15. **Could anyone relocate ahead of the family?** E.g. James moving earlier to run the business from Malta and start a residence clock, with the family following after school finishes.
16. **Other dependants or regular visitors** (e.g. Vicki's mother, given her central role in the citizenship link) whose needs should shape location or property size?

## 4. The home — location, type & livability

17. **Area preferences or no-go's.** Any Maltese localities you are drawn to or rule out? (e.g. central/Sliema–Gżira corridor, the quieter inland belt — Attard/Lija/Balzan/Mosta — the north, or Gozo.) Have you spent time on the island?

20. **Renovation appetite.** Would you consider a property needing works (often better value and growth), or do you want turnkey?
21. **Gozo vs main island.** Is ferry-dependent island life appealing or a dealbreaker?

## 5. Rental income — your appetite as landlords

*Why this matters:* the legal route and the economics differ sharply between long-let and licensed short-let, and between being hands-on and hands-off.

22. **Long-let or short-let?** Steady long-term tenancy (simpler, lower gross, 15% flat final tax option) or licensed holiday short-let (higher gross, MTA Holiday Furnished Premises licence required, more management, tighter 2026 rules)? Or open to either on our advice?
23. **Hands-on or hands-off.** Would you self-manage or use a letting/management agent (typically ~10–15% of rent)? This changes the net yield we model.
24. **One property or two?** Are you open to a "live in one, let another" two-property split (two transactions, two sets of costs), or do you prefer a single property — even if that means a dual-purpose SDA unit or deferring income?
25. **Furnishing & fit-out budget.** Is furniture/fit-out for a let unit inside the €1M, or separate?

## 6. Financing & cash position

*Why this matters:* loan-to-value, rates, and cash required differ dramatically by residency/citizenship status, and a local bank account usually gates a Maltese mortgage.

26. **🔑 Cash vs mortgage.** Do you intend to buy **outright in cash**, or use a **Maltese mortgage**? If financing, roughly what deposit are you prepared to put down? (As third-country nationals today, LTV is ~65%; as residents/citizens, up to 90% on a primary residence — a strong reason to time the purchase.)
27. **Source & currency of funds.** Are funds in AUD, GBP, EUR, or a mix? Any FX timing or transfer constraints? (Currency movement on a €1M purchase is material.) Will any funds come from selling Australian assets — and if so, are there Australian CGT consequences to time around?
28. **Existing property/assets.** Do you own property in Australia or the UK that you will keep, sell, or let? Existing debt or commitments that affect borrowing capacity?
29. **Income for serviceability.** Roughly what household/business income supports a mortgage application, and in what currency? Banks cap repayments at ~25–40% of net income.
30. **Have you opened a Maltese bank account?** It typically gates the mortgage and should be started early.

## 7. The Australian business — MoveData.io structuring 🔑

*Why this matters:* James intends to keep running MoveData (a Salesforce data-integration SaaS, ~A$1M ARR) from Malta. Moving the operator across borders creates corporate tax-residency, permanent-establishment, and exit-CGT questions that must be timed against both the move and Vicki's citizenship. These are referred to cross-border tax counsel; we need the facts to frame the structuring.

31. **🔑 Current structure.** How is MoveData held today — Australian Pty Ltd? Who are the shareholders (James solely, or jointly with Vicki / others)? Any trust, holding company, or employee shareholders?
32. **Where is the value and the work?** Where are customers, IP, key staff/contractors, and revenue collected? Is James the sole operator, or is there a team that stays in Australia?
33. **🔑 What do you want to happen to the company?** Keep it Australian-resident and run it remotely, migrate/redomicile it to Malta, set up a Maltese company (and the 6/7ths refund regime), or some hybrid? Any plan to sell the business within a few years (which sharpens exit-CGT timing)?
34. **Employees & substance.** Will you hire in Malta, contract there, or keep all staff in Australia? "Where the work happens" drives permanent-establishment and tax-residency risk.
35. **Personal tax appetite.** Are you intending to use Malta's **non-dom / remittance** basis? Do you have other worldwide income/assets (dividends, investments, super/pension) that interact with the Australia–Malta double-tax treaty (1984) and Australian tax residency rules on departure?
36. **Australian exit considerations.** Are you aware of Australian "deemed disposal" exit-CGT on ceasing Australian tax residency, and do you have an Australian adviser engaged on timing the cessation against the move?
37. **Existing advisers.** Do you already have Australian accountants/tax counsel and any Maltese advisers we should coordinate with, or do you want introductions?
