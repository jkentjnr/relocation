## 0. What this document is

The revised mandate ([MAL-2](/MAL/issues/MAL-2)) re-centres the engagement on **one dual-purpose property**: a home the family will live in, that the client may **buy up to ~3 years early and let during the interim** (Q3/Q13/Q24), then occupy when the children finish school (~2029). **Base case: Vicki is a registered Maltese (EU) citizen**; both regimes (pre- and post-citizenship) are shown.

This is the **legal feasibility layer**. It does two things:
1. Resolves *the crux* — whether a property can legally be **let before it becomes the primary residence**, under each regime — and shows that the answer is route-driven, not citizenship-driven.
2. Provides the **per-property classification template** (§7) to apply to each [MAL-3](/MAL/issues/MAL-3) candidate once the shortlist lands.

Per the firm principle, this **classifies, it does not filter**: a property that cannot be let stays on the list as a *home-plus-appreciation* play.

## 2. The crux resolved — can the property be let *before* it is occupied?

The client's plan hinges on letting the property for ~3 years before moving in. Worked through both regimes:

**Pre-citizenship (both spouses third-country nationals):**
- Outside an SDA → **AIP route → letting prohibited.** Buy-early-and-let is **not available**.
- Inside an SDA → no permit, letting permitted → **available**.

**Post-citizenship (base case — Vicki is a Maltese/EU citizen):**
- The citizen **primary-residence exemption attaches to *actual use as the primary residence*.** A property bought early and **commercially let while the family still lives in Australia** is, during that window, **not her primary residence** — so the clean exemption does **not** cover it.
- A non-occupied property a citizen acquires while <5 years resident is therefore treated as a **secondary acquisition → AIP permit required → letting prohibited.** Same blocker as the pre-citizenship case, reached by a different door.
- The "let freely, any purpose" right unlocks only at **5 years' continuous residence**, which the client **cannot begin early** (Q11). Unavailable in the interim.
- Inside an **SDA** → no permit needed by anyone, letting permitted in either regime → **available**.

> **Conclusion (robust across both regimes): only an SDA property cleanly supports "buy early and let during the ~3-year interim."** Citizenship does **not** rescue the interim let on a non-SDA property — the exemption is keyed to occupation, which by definition hasn't happened yet. *This must be confirmed by a Maltese notary/advocate; do not rely on a "declared future primary residence" argument while the unit is tenanted and the owners are non-resident.*

## 3. The four strategic routes that fall out of §2

Every [MAL-3](/MAL/issues/MAL-3) candidate will land in one of these. **None of them disqualifies a property — they classify it.**

| Route | What it is | Buy early & let? | Becomes the home? | Best for |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **A — SDA, buy early & let** | An SDA apartment/penthouse bought now, let (long or short, §6) for ~3 yrs, then occupied | **Yes** (either regime) | Yes | The client's stated "buy early only if it carries itself" condition (Q3/Q5/Q13) |
| **B — Non-SDA, defer to occupation** | A non-SDA home bought at/near the ~2029 move, as Vicki's **citizen primary residence** | No (not bought yet) | **Yes — no permit, buy anywhere** | The character-home-with-garden brief, if the client is willing to wait (Q12: open to renting meanwhile) |
| **C — Non-SDA, buy early as home-plus-appreciation** | A non-SDA home bought early but **held, not let** (AIP/secondary route — letting barred) | No (sits unlet) | Yes | A home they love that appreciates — **but conflicts with Q13 "never vacant"** |
| **D — SDA, buy at occupation** | An SDA unit bought at the move and lived in | n/a | Yes | If they like SDA living but don't need the early-let |

**The decision this forces (headline for Strategy Editor / [MAL-6](/MAL/issues/MAL-6)):**
The "buy early **and** let to carry itself" condition selects hard for **Route A → an SDA unit**, which is typically a **modern apartment or penthouse**. But the home brief wants **garden/roof/outdoor space, guest capacity, walkability to a village square, renovation-acceptable character** (Q4/Q16/Q19/Q20) — which points to **non-SDA townhouses / houses of character → Route B or C**, which *cannot* be let in the interim.

So the real client choice is:
- **Character home, deferred** (Route B) — wait until ~2029, rent a home meanwhile, then buy clean as a citizen; **forgoes interim rent**; or
- **SDA unit, early-let** (Route A) — buy now, let it carrying-cost-positive, occupy later; **but accept an apartment/penthouse, not the garden character-home**.

A property cannot generally be **both** an early-let income unit **and** the character garden-home — those are different routes and usually different property types. This is the single most important feasibility finding for the memo, and it should be put to the client as the framing trade-off, not buried.

## 4. James's residence route — confirmed

James is a **third-country national** for property purposes (Australian; post-Brexit British is a third country). His **residence** route, however, runs cleanly through Vicki once she is a registered Maltese/EU citizen:

- As the **spouse of an EU/Maltese citizen**, James is entitled to a **Residence Card as a family member** under EU free-movement rules (Directive 2004/38/EC; Maltese eResidence / Identità). This grants **residence, the right to work without a separate permit, and Schengen travel** — confirmed at runtime (§9).
- **Property:** James does **not** acquire the citizen property exemption himself. On a **joint purchase of the couple's primary residence**, the exemption relied on is **Vicki's** (citizen, primary residence). The notary must confirm **joint-acquisition treatment** so James's third-country status does not re-trigger an AIP requirement on the family home. Inside an SDA this is moot — no permit for anyone.

## 5. The 1 August 2028 descent cliff — gating dependency (outside our remit)

The entire base case ("Vicki as a registered Maltese citizen") depends on a registration that has a hard deadline:

- Under the Maltese Citizenship Act, where the qualifying ancestor was **alive on 1 Aug 2007** and **dies after 1 Aug 2028 without registering**, the descent link **breaks** and the right is lost (verified §9). Vicki's route runs through her **mother (the living link)** — so **the mother's registration must be completed before the cliff.**
- This is **specialist citizenship counsel's** work, **outside this firm's remit**, but it **gates the whole property regime**: no registered citizenship → the base case collapses to the pre-citizenship (both-TCN) column, where the home itself needs AIP or SDA.
- Status (open item **O2** on MAL-2): the client is "meeting Komunità Malta on Monday," but **Komunità Malta is the government agency, not necessarily counsel managing the mother's registration**. It is **unconfirmed whether a specialist lawyer is engaged on Vicki's mother's registration** against the 2028 deadline. **Recommendation: the client must confirm a specialist citizenship lawyer is retained on the *mother's* registration now** — this is the most time-critical dependency in the file and we should flag it to the client (Settlement & Logistics to track the residence-card mechanics once citizenship lands).

## 6. Long-vs-short let — advice (applies to Route A / SDA only)

Interim letting is only on the table for an **SDA** unit (§2). For that unit, long-vs-short:

**Short-let** requires an **MTA Holiday Furnished Premises licence** under **Legal Notice 92 of 2026** (published 15 Apr 2026, in force ~mid-June 2026). Verified rules:
- **No person may operate — or even advertise — without a valid licence.** Unlicensed operation triggers a **3-year disqualification attaching to both the operator *and* the address** (the MTA refuses any application for that address during the ban).
- **2 persons per bedroom**; total cap **10 persons** unless independent street access; **no bedrooms in basements/underground**; **high standard of finish** (poorly maintained units rejected/renewals refused); **no single booking > 90 consecutive days**; a **24-hour contact person** must be designated.
- A short-let income assumption is **invalid** for any candidate that cannot obtain and keep this licence — to be tested per property in §7.

**Recommendation:** for a *buy-early-and-let-then-occupy* plan, **long-let is the lower-risk default** — simpler compliance, a single tenant for the ~3-yr window, clean vacant-possession at occupation, no MTA licensing/finish/management overhead. **Short-let** is justified only where (a) the SDA unit is in a strong tourism micro-market, (b) it comfortably meets the LN 92/2026 finish and occupancy rules, and (c) the client accepts active management (letting agent, Q23) and the 90-day booking cap. Investment Strategy ([MAL-5](/MAL/issues/MAL-5)) to model both net-of-cost for any SDA candidate; the licensability gate (§7 row 4) is binding before any short-let yield is credited.

| # | Field | How to classify |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **SDA or non-SDA?** | Check against the statutory SDA list; this sets everything below |
| 2 | **Buy early?** — pre-citizenship | SDA = yes (no permit); non-SDA = yes but **as residence only, AIP, no let** |
| 2b | **Buy early?** — post-citizenship | SDA = yes; non-SDA = home only if occupied, else AIP-secondary (no let) |
