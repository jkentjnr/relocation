## 2. Headline: two answers change the engagement

The discovery answers do more than fill gaps — two of them re-shape the mandate the 18 June memo was built on.

### 2.1 🔴 ONE property, not two — the "live-one / let-one" split is off the table
The 18 June memo's **recommended** strategy (Strategy C) was a two-property "live in one, let another" split. The client has now answered, twice and unambiguously (Q3, Q24): **"One property only."** They may buy that single property up to ~3 years early *and let it in the interim* if the investment case lines up; otherwise they wait until they move.

**Consequence:** the engagement re-centres on a **single dual-purpose property** — a home they will live in, that may earn rent for an interim ~3-year window before they occupy it. The home-vs-income tension is no longer "split the budget"; it is "find one property that is an excellent home *and* lets cleanly during the gap, under the right ownership regime." This sharpens — rather than removes — the AIP-vs-SDA-vs-citizenship analysis (see §4).

### 2.2 🔴 The business is being SOLD (A$5–7M), not run from Malta
The brief framed MoveData as a company James would **keep running from Malta**, and the questionnaire asked how to structure it cross-border. The client's answer to Q31 changes this entirely: **"We are currently looking to sell the business for A$5M to A$7M."** A sell-side data room exists at `github.com/jkentjnr/movedata-docs-sale/tree/main/dataroom` (9 sections: corporate/transaction perimeter, financials, tax, customers/GTM, product/tech/security, IP, people, legal/compliance, process materials).

**Consequence — three-fold:**
1. The business work-strand flips from *"structure ongoing operations / permanent-establishment / 6-7ths refund"* to **exit-CGT timing on a trade sale**, sequenced against (a) ceasing Australian tax residency and (b) Vicki's Maltese citizenship / the family's move. This is the single largest financial event in the file — materially larger than the property.
2. **Funding is in question.** The client says "probably a Maltese mortgage" (Q26) and "not sure about sales to make it work" (Q27), yet a A$5–7M (~€3.0–4.3M) sale would comfortably fund a €1M cash purchase several times over. We must not model a 65%-LTV non-resident mortgage if the real picture is a cash buyer post-exit. The cash-vs-mortgage base case must be reconciled (see §6, Q1).
3. The "we have configured our business as tax-effectively as logically possible" answer to Q4 sits in tension with "seeking a clear plan" (Q35) and the live sale — so the **timing of the sale relative to the departure date is itself a planning lever** we should surface, not assume settled.

> **Net:** the engagement is now *one property + one liquidity event*, where the timing of the business sale, the cessation of Australian tax residency, and Vicki's citizenship registration all need to be sequenced on a single timeline. That timeline is the spine of the revised memo.

## 4. The legal hinge, restated for a single property

With one property that may be **let before it becomes the primary residence**, the AIP/SDA/citizenship question is now the crux — and it must be *classified*, not used to filter:

- A Maltese citizen's **no-permit, buy-anywhere** exemption attaches to a **primary residence**. A property bought early and **let for ~3 years before the family occupies it** is, during that window, **not yet their primary residence** — so the clean citizen exemption may not cover the *letting* phase. Letting on any property unlocks freely only after **5 years' continuous residence** (which the client cannot start early — Q11: "No").
- This makes the **interim-let phase the legally awkward part**, and it points hard at either (a) an **SDA property** (lets freely in either regime, multiple/any-purpose permitted, no 5-year wait) for the buy-early-and-let plan, or (b) **deferring the purchase** to coincide with occupation as a citizen (Q12: client is open to renting a home in the meantime).
- **Director of Relocation & Legal must classify each shortlisted option on this exact point:** can this specific property be (i) bought now, (ii) let during the gap, and (iii) become the family home — and under which regime and at what permit cost. This is the question that decides whether "buy early" is even available, and it is property-specific.

The AIP-vs-SDA decision is therefore surfaced **early as a trade-off to decide**, framed around the interim-let, not as a gate.
