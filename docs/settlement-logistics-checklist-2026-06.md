## 1. BANKING & FUNDING THE PURCHASE

### 1.1 The real constraint

A Maltese bank account is **hard for a non-resident TCN and easy for a resident**. Banks routinely decline or stall non-residents without a residence permit, and demand extra proof of ties to Malta, source of funds, a home-bank reference, and proof of purpose; due-diligence refusals come without explanation, and timelines run weeks to months ([FreeMalta](https://freemalta.com/know/malta-bank-account), [Lawyers Malta](https://lawyersmalta.eu/open-a-bank-account-in-malta/), [ImmigrantInvest](https://immigrantinvest.com/blog/malta-banks/)).

- **Before citizenship/residence (Track A):** opening a personal account at a Maltese retail bank (BOV, APS, etc.) as two non-resident Australians is the friction point — possible but slow and not guaranteed. Their answer "waiting for citizenship" (Q30) is, for the *account*, the path of least resistance.
- **After Vicki's citizenship + the family-member card for James (Track B):** both become residents → standard resident account opening, far smoother. **This is the natural time to open the operating account.**

### 1.2 KEY FINDING — you do NOT need a Maltese bank account to complete a cash purchase

The conveyancing rails run through the **notary's client account**, not the buyer's Maltese account:

- On signing the **konvenju** (promise of sale / preliminary agreement) the buyer pays a **~10% deposit held in escrow by the notary**; the **balance is wired to the notary's client account in time for the final deed** ([Mondaq / Attard Baldacchino](https://www.mondaq.com/real-estate/1624232/legal-considerations-for-non-residents-buying-property-in-malta-source-of-funds-notarial-research-and-the-konvenju-process), [GVZH](https://gvzh.mt/services/practice-areas/property-real-estate/purchasing/)).
- Funds can be sent **directly from an overseas (Australian) account to the notary's client account** — the buyer never needs a local bank account to *complete*. (This is the same rail used for crypto-funded purchases, which route converted EUR into the notary's client account.)

> **Sequencing answer:** **Yes — they can fund and complete a purchase before holding any Maltese account.** The notary's client account is the receiving vehicle. The Maltese personal account is a *settling-in* convenience (ARMS direct debits, salary/rent, day-to-day), best opened once resident — not a prerequisite to buy.

### 1.3 Source-of-funds / AML on a large AUD inflow (load-bearing — START EARLY)

Non-resident buyers must evidence the **legal origin of all funds** to the notary (and any bank), via bank statements, business-income proof, investment records ([Mondaq](https://www.mondaq.com/real-estate/1624232/legal-considerations-for-non-residents-buying-property-in-malta-source-of-funds-notarial-research-and-the-konvenju-process)). A ~€1M inflow traced to an **A$5–7M business sale** will draw maximal scrutiny.

**Assemble a contemporaneous dossier** (coordinate with the cross-border tax/M&A advisers running the MoveData sale): share purchase agreement / sale contract, completion statement, the receiving Australian bank statements showing proceeds in, prior tax returns, and a clean **fund-flow narrative** (business sale → Australian account → FX conversion → notary client account). Because the sale and the purchase may be ~3 years apart, **capture the SOF paper now** so the trail is unbroken at completion.

### 1.4 FX: AUD→EUR on ~€1M (a real cost line)

The transfer is **AUD-denominated funds → EUR** — currency risk and spread are a live cost, not a footnote:

- **Use a specialist currency broker, not the retail bank.** Brokers offer materially better AUD/EUR rates than banks on large property transfers and can advise on timing ([MoneySavingExpert](https://www.moneysavingexpert.com/banking/foreign-currency-exchange/), [GoodMoneyGuide](https://goodmoneyguide.com/fx/currency-brokers/)). On ~€1M, even ~1% of spread is ~€10k.
- **Currency risk over the gap:** if the sale completes (AUD in hand) well before the deed, AUD/EUR can move materially. A **forward contract** can lock the rate between sale proceeds and completion. Flag to Investment Strategy as a hedging decision, not just an admin step.
- Send via a regulated provider with a clean SOF position so the EUR lands in the notary client account without an AML hold.

## 2. RESIDENCY REGISTRATION (Track B — post-citizenship)

**Dependency chain — strict order:**

```
Vicki's citizenship REGISTERED (gated by mother's registration; 1 Aug 2028 cliff)
        │
        ▼
Vicki present in Malta + exercising EU treaty rights
(employed / self-employed / economically self-sufficient)  ──► her eResidence / ID
        │
        ▼
James applies as FAMILY MEMBER (non-EU spouse of EU citizen) under Dir. 2004/38/EC
        │
        ▼
James's family-member eResidence card
```

### 2.1 Vicki (Maltese/EU citizen)
Once citizenship is registered she obtains her **Maltese ID / eResidence** via Identità's Expatriates Unit as an EU national. To **sponsor James**, she must be exercising treaty rights — employed, self-employed, **economically self-sufficient**, or studying ([Identità — EU Nationals](https://identita.gov.mt/expatriates-unit-main-page/eu-nationals/)). Given the post-sale liquidity, **"economically self-sufficient" (self-sufficiency + comprehensive sickness insurance)** is the obvious basis. **She must establish her own position before James's card can issue** — this is the sequencing crux.

### 2.2 James (TCN spouse — family member route)
Via Identità Expatriates Unit, **family-member eResidence** under Directive 2004/38/EC. Documents per Identità ([Family Member page](https://identita.gov.mt/expatriates-unit-main-page/eu-nationals/eresidence-document-application/family-member/)):

- Full passport copy (TCN: **complete** copy, not just bio page)
- **Original + copy of the sponsor's (Vicki's) passport and residence document**
- **Original + copy of the marriage certificate** (married 7 years — ample) proving the relationship
- **Health insurance, min. €100,000** Malta medical coverage
- Forms **CEA F.01/F.02 + GDPR form**, via the [Expatriates Unit Portal](https://expatriates.identita.gov.mt/)
- All foreign documents **translated to EN/MT and legalised/apostilled**

**Timeline:** ~**30 working days** once a complete application is filed ([Identità](https://identita.gov.mt/expatriates-unit-main-page/eu-nationals/eresidence-document-application/family-member/)).

### 2.3 Before vs after the move
- **Before move (now → ~2029):** get the Australian **marriage certificate apostilled**, passports valid with long runway, and arrange the **€100k+ health insurance** to be in force on arrival. Pre-stage Vicki's self-sufficiency evidence.
- **After move (on arrival ~2029):** Vicki registers first; James files the family-member application immediately after. The **TIN, resident bank account, and resident ARMS tariff all flow from residence** — so cluster them right after the cards issue.

## 3. UTILITIES & SETUP (ARMS + connectivity)

### 3.1 ARMS account (water + electricity)
On acquiring a property already connected, the new owner files a **Change of Consumer / Form F** (change of ownership) — or **Form H** (change of account holder) — with ID, the deed/contract, and proof of residence ([Yitaku ARMS guide](https://www.yitaku.com/post/how-to-register-utilities-in-malta-arms-setup-guide-for-new-homeowners), [servizz.gov.mt Form F](https://www.servizz.gov.mt/en/Pages/Environment_-Energy_-Agriculture-and-Fisheries/Energy/Energy-and-Water-Services/WEB289/default.aspx)). The account **must be in place before any interim tenant moves in** (Track A).

### 3.2 The tariff difference — material if owned-but-let or owned-but-vacant
Three tariff classes ([Expatax](https://expatax.mt/utilities-arms-tariffs-and-resident-rates/), [ARMS tariffs](https://arms.com.mt/en/information/water-and-electricity/tariff-prices), [Invoice Data Extraction](https://invoicedataextraction.com/blog/malta-arms-residential-domestic-non-residential-tariff)):

| Tariff | Applies when | Effect |
|---|---|---|
| **Residential** | Persons are **registered as ordinarily resident** at the premises | **Lowest rates**, plus per-person **eco-reduction** below consumption thresholds |
| **Domestic** | A dwelling with **no registered residents** | **Higher** than Residential |
| **Non-Residential** | Not registered as Domestic or Residential | **Highest** |

Tariff follows **who is registered at the property**, not who owns or pays ([Invoice Data Extraction](https://invoicedataextraction.com/blog/malta-arms-residential-domestic-non-residential-tariff)).

**Implications across the gap:**
- **Owned-but-vacant** (no one registered): defaults to **Domestic** at best — higher rates, no eco-reduction. The review already says: don't leave it vacant (Q13).
- **Owned-but-let long-term:** the **tenant** registers as ordinarily resident → tenant gets the Residential tariff and bills (clean separation; agent handles).
- **Owned-but-short-let:** no resident registers → **Domestic/Non-Residential** rates apply, and the owner typically carries utilities inside the nightly price. A cost line for the short-let model.
- **Once the Kents occupy as residents (~2029):** they register as ordinarily resident → switch to **Residential** + eco-reduction. **Register their residence at the property promptly on arrival** to capture the lower tariff and the per-person discount.

### 3.3 Internet / fibre
Order **fibre** (GO, Melita, Epic) once the ARMS supply and an address are live; installation lead times are routine but should be booked ahead of any tenant or of their own arrival ([MyRent utilities](https://www.myrent.mt/guides/utilities-setup)). For a short-let, **reliable Wi-Fi is effectively mandatory** for listings/guests.

## 4. MOVING & IMPORTING (Track B — sequence around the move)

### 4.1 Household goods — Transfer-of-Residence (ToR) customs relief
Moving from Australia (third country), they can claim **relief from import duty/VAT** on used household/personal effects, **but the conditions are exacting** ([MTCA Customs](https://mtca.gov.mt/customs/individual/importation-into-malta-of-used-household-and-personal-belongings-upon-transfer-of-residence-from-a-non-eu-country), [Malta Customs ToR](https://customs.gov.mt/mc/individuals/transfer-of-residence)):

- Normal residence **outside the EU for a continuous 12 months** before transfer (proof from a competent authority required). ✅ satisfied — Melbourne base.
- Goods **owned and used** at the former residence for **≥6 months** before ceasing third-country residence.
- Import window: from **6 months before** establishing residence in the EU to **12 months after** establishing residence in Malta.
- **No relief** for alcohol, tobacco, foodstuffs, restricted goods.

> **Sequencing flag:** ToR relief is tied to **establishing residence**, which is gated by citizenship/cards (§2). Don't ship the container until the residence position is in train and within the 6-months-before → 12-months-after window. Use a mover experienced in Australia→Malta sea freight; build in **8–12+ weeks transit** from Melbourne.

### 4.2 Vehicle — probably NOT worth importing, despite RHD/left-side driving
- **Good news:** Malta **drives on the left, like Australia → right-hand-drive is the norm**, so an Australian car is the correct hand (unlike most of Europe) ([Expat Focus](https://www.expatfocus.com/malta/guide/malta-buying-or-importing-a-car)).
- **But the relief and approval hurdles bite:** the **Motor Vehicle Registration Tax exemption** on ToR requires the vehicle to have been **registered in the owner's name for ≥24 continuous months** before transfer **and** the owner to have lived outside Malta ≥24 months ([Ministry for Finance ToR](https://finance.gov.mt/tore_main_page/), [servizz.gov.mt](https://www.servizz.gov.mt/en/Pages/Transport-and-Communications/Transport-Services/Land-Transport/WEB734/default.aspx)).
- A vehicle imported from **outside the EU** needs a **Single Vehicle Approval (SVA)** for EU emissions/safety eligibility; the whole declare-inspect-register process must complete **within 30 days** of the car arriving ([Transport Malta](https://www.transport.gov.mt/land/vehicles/registering-and-licensing-a-motor-vehicle/registering-a-vehicle-documents-required-3586)).

> **Recommendation:** for an ordinary Australian car, the **SVA + freight + 30-day registration scramble usually outweighs the saved registration tax**. The RHD compatibility is a genuine point, but Malta is small and parking-tight — **buy a used RHD car locally on arrival** unless a specific vehicle has sentimental/special value. Flag as a client choice; if they want to import, the **24-month ownership clock must be confirmed early**.

### 4.3 PETS — START EARLY (longest non-negotiable lead time in the file)
**Assume they may have a dog/cat** (to confirm with client — O-item). Entry from a third country into the EU/Malta under the **new EU animal health framework in force from 22 April 2026** ([Across the Pond Pet](https://www.acrossthepondpet.com/resources/blog/169-eu-pet-travel-rules-2026-new-regulations-certificate-deadlines-what-usa-pet-owners-need-to-know), [Pet Travel — Malta](https://www.pettravel.com/information/pet-passports/malta-pet-import-requirements/), [EU Food Safety](https://food.ec.europa.eu/animals/live-animal-movements/dogs-cats-and-ferrets/bringing-pet-eu-non-eu-country_en)):

1. **ISO 11784/11785 microchip FIRST** — must precede the rabies vaccination, or the vaccine is invalid and must be redone.
2. **Rabies vaccination** after the chip; valid only **≥21 days** after administration.
3. **Rabies antibody titre test (RNATT)** — sample taken **≥30 days after vaccination**, at an approved lab; then a **3-month (90-day) wait** from a passing result before EU entry.
4. **EU Animal Health Certificate** issued shortly before travel.
5. **Dogs into Malta: mandatory Echinococcus (tapeworm) treatment 24–120 hours before arrival.**

> **Total chain: ~4–7 months minimum** with no slippage, and titre-test scheduling slack pushes it longer. **Begin ≥7 months before the intended pet travel date.** This is the single item most likely to derail a clean family move if left late. Confirm pet ownership with the client now and, if yes, set the microchip/vaccine clock as the **earliest action item** in the whole settlement plan.

## 5. INTERIM-LET LOGISTICS (Track A — what must be in place *remotely*)

If they buy early and let via an agent while still in Melbourne, the following must exist **before a tenant/guest moves in**, all manageable remotely:

| Item | Long-let | Short-let | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Letting-agent mandate** | ✔ | ✔ | Written management mandate; agent collects rent, handles tenants, remits net. Client confirmed agent-managed, not self (Q23). |
| **ARMS account (Form F/H)** | ✔ (then tenant registers) | ✔ (owner carries utilities) | §3. For long-let the **tenant** registers → Residential tariff; for short-let it stays **Domestic/Non-Residential**. |
| **MTA short-let licence** | — | ✔ **mandatory** | Required for any Airbnb/Booking/Vrbo listing; **fines up to €10k** unlicensed; **~4–6 weeks** to grant ([Casa Rooms](https://casarooms.com/malta-short-let-regulations-2026/), [Gobnb/PropertyManager](https://propertymanager.com.mt/services/mta-licence-guide-for-short-lets/)). Needs valid development permission + planning conformity. |
| **24/7 contact person** | — | ✔ **mandatory** | MTA rules: a **named natural person available 24/7** to take complaints/resolve issues ([VB Advocates](https://www.vba.mt/2026/04/23/operating-short-lets-in-malta-heres-what-the-2026-regulations-change/), [Victor Estate](https://www.victorestate.com.mt/short-let-in-malta-best-practices-for-owners-in-2025/)). **The Kents cannot be this person from Melbourne** → the **letting/management agent must contractually be the 24/7 contact.** Hard requirement, not optional. |
| **Long-let registration** | ✔ | — | Long private residential leases must be registered with the Housing Authority and meet minimum-term rules — confirm current regime with the agent. |
| **Mail / property management** | ✔ | ✔ | Agent or a local contact for post, inspections, ARMS bills, maintenance, condominium matters while non-resident. |
| **Insurance** | ✔ | ✔ | Landlord/buildings + contents; short-let may need specific cover. |
| **Banking** | optional | optional | Rent can be remitted by the agent to their **Australian account**; a Maltese account is convenient but not required during Track A (§1.1). |

> **Decision dependency:** the short-let path layers on the **MTA licence + 24/7 contact** obligations that long-let avoids — feed this into the long-vs-short-let recommendation (Q22, "open to advice"). Long-let is **operationally lighter to run remotely from Melbourne**; short-let needs a fuller management agent acting as licensed 24/7 contact.

## 7. Dependencies & flags (one-glance)

- **Citizenship is the master gate** → residency cards, resident bank account, resident ARMS tariff, ToR relief timing, and the clean ownership regime all sit downstream of it (and of the **1 Aug 2028 cliff**).
- **You can buy without a Maltese bank account** — the notary client account is the rail. Don't let "waiting for citizenship for banking" (Q30) be misread as blocking the purchase.
- **SOF must be contemporaneous** with the A$5–7M sale — the largest AML exposure in the file.
- **FX on ~€1M is a real cost + risk line** — use a broker; consider a forward over the sale-to-completion gap.
- **Pets are the longest hard lead** — confirm ownership and start the clock first.
- **Short-let ≠ long-let operationally** — short-let drags in the MTA licence + a 24/7 contact the Kents can't be from Melbourne (agent must hold it); feed into the long-vs-short recommendation.
- **Vehicle import rarely pays** despite the RHD/drive-on-left match.
