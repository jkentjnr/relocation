# Livability Assessment

Use this skill to judge whether the couple would actually enjoy living somewhere, not just whether it rents well.

## When to use
- For each shortlisted locality, alongside the financial and legal analysis.

## Method
Score each area on:
1. **Healthcare** — proximity to health centres and hospital (Mater Dei), private clinics, pharmacy access.
2. **Connectivity & transport** — bus coverage, traffic and commute reality, distance to the airport and ferry, walkability.
3. **Daily amenities** — groceries, restaurants, seafront/green space, gyms, services.
4. **Social fabric** — expat community, language (English is widely spoken and official), safety, pace of life.
5. **Nuisance factors** — noise, summer crowds, and the construction pressure that affects many high-demand areas.
6. **Seasonality** — how the area feels in peak summer vs winter, especially in tourist zones.
7. **Schooling** — only if relevant to the couple.

## Output
A per-area livability profile with a simple score and a candid "what it's like to live here year-round" narrative, explicitly tied to the dual mandate (would the couple love it *and* would tenants/guests want it).

## Caveats
Livability is partly subjective; present the evidence and the trade-offs rather than a single verdict, and recommend a scouting visit before committing.
