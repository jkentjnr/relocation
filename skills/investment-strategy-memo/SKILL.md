# Investment Strategy Memo

Use this skill to assemble the firm's one deliverable.

## When to use
- Once Market Research, Investment Strategy, and Relocation & Legal have produced their analyses.

## Method
1. **Reconcile first.** Resolve any conflict between teams before writing — most importantly, strip any rental income from candidates that Legal says cannot be let.
2. **Assemble the memo** in this structure:
   - **Executive summary & recommendation** — the recommended strategy (locality + property type + ownership route) and why, in a few lines.
   - **Client mandate** — restate the client's objectives, their priority, and budget (from the engagement brief) as the frame.
   - **The core decision** — the AIP-vs-SDA / live-and-let structure that makes the recommendation legal.
   - **Shortlist comparison** — 2–4 candidate strategies in a like-for-like table: all-in cost, net yield, cash-at-deed, legality verdict, livability.
   - **The numbers** — financing, tax, and ROI (base + downside) behind the recommendation.
   - **Risk register** — honest risks (rate, regulatory, occupancy, liquidity, construction/area) and mitigations.
   - **Action plan** — the sequenced steps from offer to keys, interlocked with the relocation logistics.
   - **Sources & verification flags** — what each figure is based on and what must be re-checked before committing money.
3. **Lead with the answer, then justify it.** Recommendation first; reasoning and numbers behind it.

## Output
A single polished memo (markdown) that a non-specialist client can read and act on. It puts the home first and treats income as a strong preference: the recommendation may be a dual-purpose property or a standout home-only property, and where income is forgone the memo states plainly what is given up and why the home justifies it. The shortlist keeps both a strong rentable option and, where one exists, a standout home-only option in view.

## Caveats
The memo is decision support, not professional financial, tax, or legal advice; it must point the client to qualified Maltese professionals for final confirmation.
