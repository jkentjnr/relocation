# Short-Let Licensing Assessment

Use this skill before any short-let income is assumed. Under Legal Notice 92 of 2026, an unlicensed short-let is prohibited.

## When to use
- Whenever a candidate's economics depend on holiday/short-term letting.

## Method
1. **Confirm a licence is required and obtainable.** No person may operate — or even advertise — a short-let without a valid MTA Holiday Furnished Premises licence.
2. **Test the property against the 2026 rules:**
   - Occupancy capped at **2 persons per bedroom**, and a total cap of **10 persons** per unit unless it has independent street access.
   - **No bedrooms in basements or underground levels.**
   - Property must meet a **high standard of finish**; the Authority can reject poorly maintained units and refuse renewals.
   - Individual bookings may not exceed **90 consecutive days**.
3. **Weigh the penalty risk.** Operating unlicensed triggers a **3-year disqualification** that attaches to *both* the operator and the specific address — the MTA will refuse any application for that address during the ban.
4. **Verdict.** Return "licensable: yes / no / conditional", with the specific conditions or blockers.

## Output
A clear licensability verdict per candidate, the conditions to satisfy, and any structural blocker (e.g. basement bedroom, finish standard, occupancy maths).

## Caveats
Regulations and legal-notice details change; confirm current MTA requirements and any condominium or local restrictions before relying on a short-let plan. This is research-grade guidance, not legal advice.
