# Investment ROI Modeling

Use this skill to make competing strategies comparable on one page.

## When to use
- Once yield, financing, tax, and legality inputs exist for the candidates.

## Method
1. **Normalise inputs.** Pull net rent (Rental Yield Analyst), financing terms (Financing Analyst), and tax treatment (Tax & Residency Advisor) into one consistent assumption set.
2. **Per-candidate model:**
   - All-in acquisition cost (price + stamp duty + notary + searches + agency).
   - Annual net cash flow (net rent − mortgage interest − operating costs − tax).
   - Net yield on all-in cost; cash-on-cash return on equity invested.
   - Total cost of ownership and a multi-year return scenario including assumed capital growth and exit costs.
3. **Respect legality.** If Legal flags a candidate as no-let (AIP residence), set its rental line to zero — it competes as a home-plus-capital play only.
4. **Structural comparison.** Model the strategic options side by side:
   - **Single dual-purpose property** (live in it; let it when away — only if licensable/legal).
   - **Live-one / let-one** (e.g. an SDA unit let for income while the client lives in a residence) within the client's budget envelope.
   - **Buy now vs rent-first-then-buy** to time the market and learn the localities.
5. **Downside case.** Re-run base assumptions under higher rates, lower occupancy, and flat prices.

## Output
A like-for-like comparison table (base + downside) across candidates and structural options, with net yield, cash-on-cash, and multi-year return, every assumption stated inline.

## Caveats
A model is only as good as its inputs and its honesty about legality and net (not gross) returns. Flag the biggest sensitivity for each option.
