# Mortgage Financing Analysis

Use this skill to turn a purchase price into a fundable financing plan.

## When to use
- Once a candidate has a price and the client's residency status is known.

## Method
1. **Establish status.** Maltese-bank lending terms differ for residents vs non-residents — loan-to-value (LTV), rates, and documentation all change. State the assumption explicitly.
2. **Determine LTV and deposit.** Estimate the maximum LTV for the client's profile and the resulting deposit. Note that a promise of sale typically requires a ~10% deposit at signing regardless of mortgage.
3. **Rate and term.** Capture an indicative rate and term, fixed vs variable options, and compute the monthly servicing cost.
4. **Cash-at-deed.** Sum deposit + acquisition costs (stamp duty, notary, searches, agency) into the real upfront cash requirement and surface it prominently.
5. **Serviceability and stress test.** Check the monthly cost against the client's income and re-run it at a higher rate (e.g. +2%) to confirm resilience.
6. **Residence vs buy-to-let.** Note where financing for an SDA investment unit differs from financing the client's own residence.

## Output
A financing summary per candidate: status assumption, LTV, deposit, indicative rate/term, monthly cost, cash-at-deed, and a stressed-rate figure.

## Caveats
Bank products and rates change frequently; re-verify with current Maltese lenders. This is research-grade guidance, not a mortgage offer.
