# Cross-Border Business Structuring (Australia ↔ Malta)

Use this skill when a foreign owner-operator (here: an Australian, also British, husband) wants to run an Australian company from Malta and needs the structure to be tax-efficient and compliant in *both* countries.

## When to use
- Any question about operating the Australian business from Malta, or the couple's overall tax footprint.

## Method

### 1. Diagnose the current exposure (where is the company taxed?)
- **Australia:** an **Australian-incorporated company is an Australian tax resident** purely by incorporation — managing it from Malta does **not** make it stop being Australian-resident. Australia taxes its worldwide income.
- **Malta:** a company **incorporated outside Malta but "managed and controlled" in Malta is resident in Malta** (as a non-domiciled company → taxed on Malta-source income and foreign income *remitted* to Malta). Separately, carrying on business *from* Malta can create a **permanent establishment (PE)** whose profits Malta can tax.
- **Conclusion to surface:** operating the Australian company day-to-day from Malta typically creates **dual exposure** — Australian residence + a Maltese PE and/or Maltese residence — which must be resolved deliberately.

### 2. Apply the Australia–Malta Double Tax Agreement (1984)
- **Dual-resident company tie-breaker** decides primary taxing rights.
- **Business profits** are taxed where the company is resident *unless* attributable to a **PE** in the other state.
- **Withholding caps:** dividends and interest **15%**, royalties **10%** — relevant to extracting profits.
- Note the treaty's shorter construction-PE threshold (183 days) if relevant.

### 3. Lay out the realistic options
1. **Keep it Australian, minimise Maltese footprint.** Hard if the husband physically works from Malta — substance of decision-making sits in Malta, so a Maltese PE/residence is likely. Often the *highest-risk* "do nothing" path.
2. **Build Maltese substance / a Maltese company.** A Malta trading company is taxed at 35% but the **6/7ths shareholder refund** brings the **effective rate to ~5%** on trading income (**5/7ths → ~10%** on passive interest/royalties). Requires genuine substance (office, decision-making, people) and careful migration of the Australian business.
3. **Hybrid.** Maltese operating company for work performed from Malta; the Australian company retained/wound down for genuinely Australian-source activity. Mind transfer pricing between them.

### 4. Layer in the personal tax position
- **Malta non-dom remittance basis:** as a non-Maltese-domiciliary resident in Malta, **foreign income not remitted to Malta is untaxed (0%)**; Malta-source income and remittances are taxed. A **minimum tax of ~€5,000/year (per married couple)** applies where foreign income exceeds €35,000 and is not fully remitted.
- **Australian exit:** ceasing Australian tax residency triggers **CGT Event I1 — a deemed disposal** of non-Australian (non-TAP) assets at market value (election to defer is available but all-or-nothing). Company shares and asset values at departure matter; **time the move and any restructure around this.**

### 5. Flag the anti-avoidance traps
- Australian **Controlled Foreign Company (CFC)** rules can attribute a foreign company's income back to Australian-resident controllers.
- Australian **Personal Services Income (PSI)** rules can look through a company where income is essentially one person's services.
- **Transfer pricing** on any intercompany charges must be arm's length.
- Malta **economic-substance** expectations — a paper structure with no real activity fails.

## Output
A structuring assessment: the current dual-exposure diagnosis, the options with their effective tax cost and compliance burden, a recommended structure, the implementation steps, and the timing interaction with the move, the wife's citizenship, and the Australian exit-CGT event.

## Caveats
This is research-grade guidance, **not** tax advice. Cross-border structuring is fact-specific and changes with law and practice — it **must** be confirmed by a **Maltese tax advisor and an Australian tax advisor jointly** before implementation. All rates, thresholds, and treaty points are 2026 reference points to be re-verified.
