# Property Legal & Conveyancing

Use this skill to confirm a candidate is legal to own and let, and to map the route to keys-in-hand.

## When to use
- For every candidate before it is allowed into the financial model.

## Method
0. **Pin down citizenship/residency status first — it sets the whole route.** The same property has a different legal route depending on who is buying and when. Use this matrix:
   - **Third-country / non-EU national** (incl. Australian, and post-Brexit British): needs an **AIP permit** outside an SDA (one residence, no letting), or buys in an **SDA** (no permit, may let, multiple allowed).
   - **Maltese / EU citizen, not yet resident 5 years:** **no permit for their primary residence**, bought anywhere (not limited to SDA). Still needs AIP for a **secondary** property (which then cannot be let), so rental income still runs cleanest through an **SDA** unit.
   - **Maltese / EU citizen resident 5+ years continuously:** may **acquire freely for any purpose — residential, rental, or investment — with no permit**.
   - **Spouse who is a third-country national but married to a Maltese/EU citizen:** has family-member residence rights; on a **joint purchase of the couple's primary residence** the citizen-spouse's primary-residence exemption is the key — confirm joint-acquisition treatment with the notary.
   For this engagement specifically: model **both regimes** — "pre-citizenship" (both third-country) and "post-citizenship" (wife Maltese/EU) — because the right structure can differ, and the recommendation should say which regime it assumes and whether to wait.
1. **Determine the ownership route** for each candidate given the status above.
   - **AIP permit (Chapter 246):** required for third-country nationals (and EU citizens not resident 5+ years buying a *secondary* property) outside an SDA. An AIP property must be used as the buyer's **residence and cannot be let**, and such a buyer may generally hold **only one**. Permit fee ≈ **€233**, issued in ~**35 days**. Minimum-value thresholds apply (reference 2026: ≈ €143,410 for apartments, ≈ €241,701 for houses/villas).
   - **Special Designated Areas (SDA):** **no AIP permit needed by anyone**, the property **may be let**, and an owner **may hold multiple** properties. This is the route that supports a combined home-and-income strategy *regardless* of citizenship. Named SDAs include Portomaso, Tigné Point, Pendergardens, Fort Cambridge, and Smart City.
   - **Maltese-citizen primary-residence exemption:** once the wife holds Maltese citizenship, the couple's **primary residence can be bought anywhere with no AIP and no SDA limitation** — the widest home search.
2. **State the verdict plainly** per candidate: *may live in (yes/no)*, *may let (yes/no)*, *route*.
3. **Check short-let licensability** where relevant (call `short-let-licensing-assessment`).
4. **Map the conveyancing path:**
   - Engage a **notary** (independent; runs searches and due diligence).
   - **Promise of sale (konvenju):** typically ~10% deposit and provisional stamp duty paid; period for searches, AIP application, and financing.
   - **AIP application** where required (≈€233, ~35 days).
   - **Final deed of sale:** balance, stamp duty, and notary fees paid; title transfers.
   - Indicative cost stack: stamp duty ~5% (relief may apply on a residence), notary ~1–2.5%, plus searches and agency.

## Output
Per candidate: the live/let verdict and route, the conveyancing roadmap with milestones, and an indicative cost-and-timeline summary.

## Caveats
SDA designations, permit fees, and timelines change. This is research-grade guidance — a Maltese notary/advocate must confirm before any commitment.
