## 1. Executive summary & recommendation

**Your single biggest lever is not a property — it is your wife's Maltese citizenship.** It changes *what you can legally buy and rent*, and it is worth far more to this plan than any individual deal. Everything below is built around it.

> **Recommended strategy — "Secure the citizenship, then split the budget" (a phased live-one / let-one):**
>
> 1. **Immediately** push your wife's mother's Maltese citizenship-by-descent **registration**, then your wife's, with a specialist citizenship lawyer. This is the gating dependency and it has a hard 2028 cliff (§3). Until it lands, you are both third-country nationals with the most restrictive buying rules.
> 2. **The home — buy it as a Maltese citizen.** Once your wife is registered, your *primary residence* escapes the AIP permit entirely: you can buy **anywhere on the island, no Special Designated Area (SDA) restriction**. This is the widest possible home search and the single best reason to time the home purchase *after* citizenship. Allocate roughly **€380k–€430k** of the budget to the home.
> 3. **The income — a dedicated rental unit.** Put roughly **€270k–€300k** into a **2-bedroom apartment in a strong-yield central area (Msida / Gżira)**, which produces meaningfully better net yield than the famous coastal names. This can be bought in an **SDA with no permit even today**, or held as a second property once you have 5 years' residence.
>
> Net effect: a home you genuinely chose (not one constrained by the SDA list) **plus** ~€8–9k/year of net rental income, inside the €700k envelope.

**Why not just buy one dual-purpose flat now?** Because buying *today*, as third-country nationals, forces you down one of two narrow paths: an AIP property (one property, must be your residence, **cannot be let** — kills the income goal) or an SDA property (works, but premium pricing means €700k buys noticeably less home). Waiting for citizenship removes that constraint from the home and lets the budget stretch further. If you are unwilling to wait, see **Strategy B** (SDA dual-purpose) in §4 — it is the best "act now" option.

**If forced to choose one goal:** you told us the home comes first and income is a strong preference, not a rule. The recommended split honours both; but if the right home alone absorbs the full budget, take it — a superior home with no rental line is an acceptable outcome and competes as a home-plus-capital-appreciation play.

## 3. The core decision: citizenship timing drives everything

Maltese law treats *living in* and *renting out* a property very differently — and treats *you* differently depending on citizenship. There are two regimes:

### Regime 1 — Today (both third-country nationals)
Post-Brexit, your **British** citizenship is **not** an EU status for Maltese property purposes; combined with Australian citizenship, you are both **third-country nationals**. Your only two routes:

- **AIP permit** (outside an SDA): one property, must be your residence, **letting not permitted**. Permit ≈ €233, ~35 days. → satisfies the home, **kills the income goal**.
- **SDA** (e.g. Tigné Point, Fort Cambridge, Portomaso, Pendergardens, Smart City): **no permit, may let, multiple allowed** — but premium pricing. → satisfies both, at a price.

### Regime 2 — After your wife is a registered Maltese (EU) citizen
- Her **primary residence needs no AIP permit and is not limited to SDAs** — buy anywhere. You, as her spouse, gain **family-member residence rights**.
- A *second* (income) property still needs care: as a Maltese citizen not-yet-5-years-resident, a second non-SDA home needs AIP (and AIP can't be let), so **income still runs cleanest through an SDA unit** — until…
- **5 years' continuous residence**, after which a Maltese citizen may buy **freely for any purpose, including letting, on any property**.

### ⚠️ The time-critical risk
Under the Maltese Citizenship Act, if the qualifying ancestor (your wife's mother) was **alive on 1 Aug 2007 and dies after 1 Aug 2028 without registering**, the descent link can **break** and the right is lost. Your wife's route runs through her mother, so **the mother's registration is urgent**. This is **citizenship-law territory — engage a specialist now**; it sits outside this firm's remit but it gates your entire favourable property regime.

## 5. The numbers behind the recommendation

### Income unit (Strategy C), base case
- Purchase ~€280,000; all-in ~€300,000 with costs.
- Long-let rent ~€1,150/month → **€13,800/yr gross** (~4.6% gross on purchase).
- Tax: Malta's **15% flat final withholding tax** on gross long-let rent (TA24) = ~€2,070. *(The alternative — progressive rates with deductions — is worth checking annually; for many landlords the 15% flat option wins.)*
- Less management/maintenance/insurance/vacancy ~€3,000.
- **Net ≈ €8,700/yr (~2.9% net on all-in cost).**

### Financing reality (drives how much cash you need)
- **As third-country nationals today:** max LTV ~**65%** for non-residents → **35%+ deposit**, term often capped ~20 years, rate ~3.5–5.5% (foreigners often +0.25–0.5%).
- **As residents/citizens:** up to **90% LTV** on a primary residence, **75%** on investment property — far less cash up front. *This is another reason to transact after citizenship/residence.*
- Banks cap repayments at ~25–40% of net income. **A local bank account is usually required and often gates the mortgage** — open one early.

### Capital growth
Maltese residential prices have grown ~3–6%/yr; new/under-construction SDA stock in growth areas has at times run higher. Treat growth as upside, not the basis of the case.

### Downside case (stress test)
- Rate +2%, occupancy −15%, prices flat for 3 years. The income unit's net yield compresses toward ~2%; the home's "return" is purely use-value + any growth. The plan still holds because the **home is the priority and is not dependent on rental performance** — the income unit is the variable part, deliberately the smaller half of the budget.

## 6. Livability (the home has to be a home)

- **Msida / Gżira / Sliema corridor:** walkable, central, strong amenities and transport, university/hospital nearby (Mater Dei, Msida) — but **traffic, noise and construction** are real and worst in the prime belt.
- **Established residential belt (Swieqi, San Ġwann, Attard, Lija, Balzan, Mosta):** quieter, family-friendly, better value per m², steady demand — a strong "home" choice if you'll value calm over seafront.
- **North (Mellieħa, St Paul's Bay):** lower prices, seasonal rental demand, more car-dependent.
- **Gozo:** lifestyle and value, but ferry-dependent — a different pace of life.
- English is official and widely spoken; healthcare is good (public + private). **Scout in person across seasons before committing** — summer in Sliema/St Julian's feels very different from winter.

## 7. Action plan (sequenced)

**Track 1 — Citizenship (start immediately; it gates everything):**
1. Engage a Maltese citizenship-by-descent lawyer.
2. **Register your wife's mother**, then your wife (mind the 1 Aug 2028 cliff).
3. Confirm your (husband's) family-member residence route as the spouse of an EU citizen.

**Track 2 — Money & feasibility (parallel):**
4. Open a Maltese bank account (gates the mortgage).
5. Get a mortgage agreement-in-principle; confirm whether to transact pre- or post-residence given the LTV difference.
6. Confirm stamp-duty relief eligibility and the 15% TA24 rental-tax election with a Maltese tax practitioner.

**Track 3 — Property (after citizenship confirmed for the home):**
7. Scouting trip; shortlist 2–3 homes (search the *whole* island now that you're not SDA-bound) and 2–3 income units in Msida/Gżira.
8. Make an offer → engage a **notary** → sign the **promise of sale (konvenju)** (~10% deposit, provisional stamp duty) → searches, AIP only if/where still needed → **final deed**.
9. If letting short-term, secure the **MTA Holiday Furnished Premises licence** *before* advertising (mandatory; occupancy and quality rules apply; operating unlicensed carries a 3-year ban on the address).

**Track 4 — Settle:** residence registration, utilities (ARMS), healthcare, the physical move (belongings, vehicle, pets).
